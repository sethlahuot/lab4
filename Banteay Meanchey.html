Welcome Banteay Meanchey

Banteay Meanchey is a province located in the northwestern part of Cambodia, bordering Thailand. Its name means "Fortress of Victory" in Khmer, with "Banteay" meaning "fortress" or "castle," and "Meanchey" meaning "victory." The province is historically significant, with several ancient temples and structures, reflecting the rich cultural and historical heritage of the Khmer Empire.
The capital of Banteay Meanchey is Serei Saophoan, also known as Sisophon. The province is an important agricultural hub and is known for its rice production. It's also a key transportation link between Cambodia and Thailand due to its proximity to the international border. One of the major border crossings is at Poipet, a city in Banteay Meanchey, which is a well-known transit point for travelers between the two countries.
